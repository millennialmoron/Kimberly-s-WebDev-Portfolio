About Me:

From first discovering how easy and fun it was to manipulate HTML to make my MySpace the best looking, most badass representation of me to designing my first full-stack, fully operational React App this past summer has been quite the journey. In college I took a year of C++, but decided I was a lost cause at the subject when my programs didn't immediately work and my all male comp sci classmates didn't seem to like me asking questions. Math (my major) came easily to me, so I stuck with that. After college, I fiddled around with web development occasionally by making websites for free for some non profits I volunteered with. Over the years, I learned css and Flash and enjoyed the side projects, but never felt compelled to go further. Until this past year.

In March of this year, I decided to move forward with a career change after working in education for 13+ years. I signed up for a 65+ hour full stack bootcamp (online) from Udemy and began to actively spend 6+ hours daily learning front and back end programming and loving every second of it. When I finished the bootcamp, I began picking small ideas for sites and apps to build. Then, to really learn and feel competent, I decided to build my first full-stack react app. It's been quite a journey of learning, but I've loved it.

Some of My Portfolio:

The Dice Game

This is a dice game I created with my bootcamp in order to demonstrate understanding of Javascript and the DOM. It was part of a challenge, but in the end, I enjoyed the idea, added in a few new details to make it more interesting for the user's experience, and simply "published" through github.

DJ Synth Pad

This is a DJ Synth Pad I created. It was based on a JS challenge from the bootcamp I did, which had us create a drum kit. I liked the idea and decided I could make something similar but more along the lines of a DJ synth pad. The project was fun to create, and I think the outcome is pretty amusing.

Simon Game

This was a challenge to create an app based on the Simon Game. I really, really enjoyed this challenge. I solved some of the problems a little differently than the teacher did in her videos, but the outcome is just as functional and fun. This was probably one of my favorite challenges to solve, just because I loved the logical issues that needed to be addressed.

Simple Blog

This is a very simple blog, meant to demonstrate understanding in connecting a server to a database, in this case using MongoDB. I actually really enjoyed this. I haven't really used this blog much (as you can tell), I'm not much of a blogger, but it was a lot of fun to create and struggle through the docs, particularly since the videos I was watching were a bit outdated and the methodology in some cases had changed.

Newsletter Sign-Up

This is a newsletter sign-up that uses APIs to connect to mailchimp. Without a doubt, this was the most frustrating project I worked on, primarily becasue so much of the setup had changed from the time of the videos I was using to the time I was attempting the project. I did however manage to eventually find solutions and get the site working correctly. I don't currently send newsletters to this list, so it's not useful to me for that purpose, but it was a great learning experience and taught me a lot about solution-seeking when things aren't working like they should.

Organizer React App

I built this project completely from scratch after completing my bootcamp. It took about two months between work and a slight detour for Covid. Most of the project actually went rather smoothly. The React components came together fairly easily and the backend logic went pretty smoothly. Things that took the most time for me to complete were getting the React end and the server end to pass data back and forth, and misunderstanding some docs that resulted in overcomplicating publishing the site to Heroku and it not working correctly for a while.
